FEMA Emergency Management
Four Foundational Phases — Continuous Lifecycle
These phases form a continuous cycle designed to minimize the impact of disasters, organize efficient operational responses, and rebuild communities afterward.

1. Mitigation

Key Goal: Prevent disasters or minimize their impacts when they do occur
  • Adopting and enforcing strict building codes and land-use regulations
  • Risk and hazard vulnerability analysis
  • Flood insurance and protective levees / flood barriers
  • Seismic retrofitting and fire-resistant construction
  • Community hazard mapping and early warning systems

2. Preparedness

Key Goal: Build and enhance capabilities to manage emergencies effectively
  • Writing Emergency Operations Plans (EOP) and Major Emergency Response Plans (MERP)
  • Conducting disaster drills, tabletops, and full-scale exercises
  • Setting up warning systems and communication plans
  • Building CERT and community emergency response capability
  • Training and equipping emergency response teams (ERT / IMT)
  • Mutual aid agreements with neighbouring facilities / jurisdictions

3. Response

Key Goal: Stabilize the incident and prevent further loss of life or property damage
  • Activating Emergency Operations Centres (EOC) / Emergency Control Centres (ECC)
  • Search and rescue operations — ICS/NIMS structure
  • Deploying emergency medical personnel and establishing triage
  • Opening shelters, providing mass care and public warnings
  • METHANE/SMEAC reporting — inter-agency coordination
  • Regulatory notifications (OPRED, HSE, Coastguard, EPA)

4. Recovery

Key Goal: Return systems and infrastructure to normal or improved standards
  • Debris removal and site remediation
  • Rebuilding homes, infrastructure and critical facilities
  • Federal/government assistance programmes (FEMA grants, insurance)
  • Economic revitalization and community restoration
  • Incident investigation and lessons learned review
  • Updating emergency plans based on after-action findings
Reference: FEMA ICS 300 | E/L/G 0300 Intermediate ICS for Expanding Incidents | fema.gov
